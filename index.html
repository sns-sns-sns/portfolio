032 When The Ear Tells The Eye

LLC

Poster Design

2023

Concrete sound mirror, benches and activation transmission by LLC (Emiddio Vasquez and Peter Eramian)

LLC (Emiddio Vasquez & Peter Eramian) invite you to Asomatos, Limassol, to encounter a concrete sculptural environment cast in-ground and extracted on site, amongst the orange grove trees of Morir Soñando. Following multiple collaborations since 2016, this is their first land work together as LLC, introducing a new framework for their joint research and practice endeavors.

‘When the ear tells the eye where to look’ interweaves histories of the land, heritage and colonialism to resuscitate alternative modes of listening. LLC presents an environment of ritual, tributing the ear and grounding the act of listening as more than just passive auditory perception, towards deep listening as a social practice that mirrors what Ivan Illich calls “tools for conviviality”. With fragments of broken Cypriot ceramic money-banks – a saving habit of older generations – used to suspend the rebar and then congealed into the concrete sculptures, further suspending the current logics of value production, exchange and extraction.

029 Amanda

Olga Micińska / Maria Editions

Book Design

2022

Visit Website

Amanda is an artist book by Olga Micińska made in the framework of The Building Institute, an experimental platform aiming to strengthen the position of femmes builders in the domain of technical construction work. Amanda brings together literary texts by Maria Toumazou, Samantha McCulloch, Sepideh Karami and Madeleine Morley, combining fiction stories with visual artwork.

Inspired by the "Tradeswomen: magazine for women in blue-collar work" published in the 80's and 90's in the United States, Amanda is a periodical discussing matters of technology and industry from a feminist (not only female) perspective. The first issue contains fiction stories of an emancipatory character, citing trade associations, oil industry in Iran and ghosts of the printer feeders.

Publication of the first issue of Amanda is kindly supported by Mondriaan Fonds and Amsterdams Fonds voor de Kunst.

024 Anthograms

Mary Plant

Book Design

2023

Inspired by Meleager's Stephanus and his association of poetry with flowers, anthograms, an alphabet based on fruit, trees and flowers was created in 2000 to provide text for a series of paintings "Aphrodite's Garden". A wish to write these texts in a new 'language' relevant to the Aphrodite led me to a further stufy of ancient Greek poetry and to research the connection between Aphrodite/Love/Fertility with flowers and fruit.

A number of anthogram poems have been sent to poets all over the world who were asked to put them into language, to interpret them. The aim was the creation of a multilingual anthology in which, not only the anthogram poems but most poems would be read visualy by individual readeers who would probably only know very few of the languages. So far there have been poems written in twenty languages.

Anthograms is a limited-edition artists' book published on the occasion of Mary Plant's retrospective exhibition curated by Marina Christodoulidou and Marina Demosthenous.

023 The Broken Pitcher

GzZK Leipzig / Thkio Ppalies / Beirut Art Center / Galerie für Zeitgenössische Kunst / Lenbachhaus

Movie Branding / Publication Design / Poster Design / Movie Titles

2023

The crucial occurrence of The Broken Pitcher (brokenpitcher.net) is a meeting at a bank in Nicosia, Cyprus, in 2019, deciding over the foreclosure of a family house. The negotiation between bank employees and the affected family is reenacted in a short video based on memory protocols of those involved. The foreclosure is one of many on the island and is a result of the agreement between Cyprus and the Troika (EC, ECB and IMF).

The filmed responses that are shown encompass perspectives of people from different interest groups in Cyprus and beyond, including housing rights activists, people who share similar fates, public figures, lawyers, economists and artists contextualising debt and tributes in relation to the micro- and macro effects of colonial history.

The Broken Pitcher is conceived and organised by Natascha Sadr Haghighian, Marina Christodoulidou and Peter Eramian, and co-produced by Thkio Ppalies and GfZK in Leipzig.

021 The River Speaks

Harokopio University of Athens

UI & UX Design / Branding

Ongoing

This will be an educational and recreational mobile application on the cultural history of the Pediaios river. It will navigate users along the riverside park from its starting point in downtown Nicosia and will be suitable for use by the general public. It will encourage users to slow down and observe the river environment as well as provide more in-depth information on the history and ecology of the river and its interaction with people through time. The app will include a variety of sensory prompts and activities as well as rich archival visual material.

The mobile application A River Speaks is part of a two-year research project funded by the Sylvia Ioannou Foundation and has been designed by Despo Pasia and Evanthia Tselika.
